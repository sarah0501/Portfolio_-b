About

In order to find real virgin forests in Europe today, you have to use a magnifying glass search. There are 160 million hectares of forest on our continent, but only 0.6 percent of them are unaffected by humans. The few forest Estates that do justice to the title of jungle are often inadequately protected and are constantly shrinking. In Eastern Europe in particular, many primeval forests are acutely endangered by illegal clearing. Most of the remaining forests will be converted into monotonous forest forests. This means that the most important properties are lost. Sarah Latka took a closer look at these primeval worlds in her book The Last Primeval Forests of Europe. In 60 pages it explains what is meant by primeval forests, why they are so special and important, how they came about, and what changes we can expect in the future. There is also a small directory of the last primeval forest that existed in Europe in her book. In addition to this factual information, she invites you through handmade kitchen lithographs.
